Eckert IV

  • Usage

    • Usage:*

            In 1906 Max Eckert (1868-1938)of Keil, Germany, presented a set of six new global projections in which all poles are lines half as long as the Equator. Numbers 4 and 6 are of the most significant. No's 2,4, and 6 are equal-area projections, and no's 1,3, and 5 are not equal-area, but have equally spaced parrallels. The Eckert 4 or (more commonly reffered with the Roman numeral IV) projection is commonly used in the US. It ranked third as an equal-area world map projection in U.S. textbooks between 1940 and 1960.

            The meridians on no.'s 3 and 4 are elliptical arcs, with the exception of the straight central meriadians,and semicircular outer meridians. Parrallels are unequally spaced straight lines parrallel to each other. Poles are straight lines half the length of the equator. Additionally, the scale is true along the 30 ° 40' N and S.

    * Usage information source:

    Snyder, John P. Map Projections - A Working Manual Paper U.S. Geological Survey Professional Paper 1395. Washington: United States Government Printing Office, 1987.
