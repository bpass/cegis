    • Hammer-Wagner

    Usage: *

            Developed by Kalheinz Wagner of Germany in 1941, the Hammer-Wagner projection is widely used for world maps, such as climatic maps prepared by the U.S. Department of Commerce. A modified form of the Hammer projection; the poles correspond to the 65th parrallels on the Hammer, and meridians are repositioned.

     * Usage information source: An Album of Map Projections, USGS Proffesional Paper 1453
