Decision Support System for Map Projections of Small Scale Data

  • Usage

    • Peters Cylindrical Equal Area (44 degrees)

    Usage: *

            The Peters projection is a cylindrical equal-area projection that de-emphasizes area exaggerations in high latitudes by shifting the standard parallels to 44 degrees.

            The Peters projection respects the areas of things on the sphere: two portions of the sphere's surface, of equal area, will be projected onto portions of the map having equal areas; generally, the ratios between areas of portions of the sphere are equal to the ratios between the areas of the corresponding portions of the projected image of the sphere.

            It shows you accurately the amount of land area in different countries, even if it changes their geometry somewhat.
