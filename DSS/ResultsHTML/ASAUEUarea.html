Suggested Projection:

Albers Equal Area Conic

skip to other suggestions

Usage: *

     The Albers is the projection exclusively used by the USGS for sectional maps of all 50 States of the United States in the National Atlas of 1970, and for other US Maps at scales 1:2,500,000 and smaller. Like other normal conics, Albers Equal-Area Conic projection (shown above) has concentric arcs of circles for parallels and equally spaced radii as meridians. The parallels are not equally spaced, but they are farthest appart in the latitudes between the standard parallels and closer together to the North and South. The Scale factor along the parallels is too small between the standard parallels and too large beyond them, and in fact the scale factor along the meridians is the reciprocal of the scale factor along parallels, to maintain equal area.
